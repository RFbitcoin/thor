What Is THOR?

THOR is a self-hosted Bitcoin trading intelligence platform designed for serious retail traders who want institutional-quality signal analysis without the institutional price tag. Built on Python and Flask, it runs continuously and aggregates data from multiple sources into a single, actionable dashboard.

The name THOR reflects the platform's approach: decisive, data-driven, and built to cut through market noise. Rather than offering hundreds of overlapping indicators, THOR consolidates the signals that consistently matter into one 35-point composite score that tells you whether conditions favour buying, holding, or stepping aside.

THOR integrates directly with Kraken for live trading, includes a full paper trading simulator for strategy testing, and monitors macro conditions — VIX, the US Dollar Index, M2 money supply — alongside on-chain Bitcoin metrics in real time.

The 35-Point Signal System

THOR's core is a composite scoring engine that evaluates Bitcoin market conditions across eleven independent pillars. Each pillar is scored independently and combined into a single 35-point score. Scores above 20 indicate bullish conditions; scores above 28 indicate strong buy conditions; scores below 12 suggest caution or avoidance.

35 pts
Maximum composite signal score. The system evaluates eleven independent market pillars — technical momentum, macro conditions, on-chain health, volatility regime, and more — weighting each based on historical significance. No single indicator can produce a false signal alone.
  • 01

    RSI Momentum — 14-period Relative Strength Index measuring overbought and oversold conditions across multiple timeframes.

  • 02

    Moving Average Trend — 50/200 EMA alignment, Golden Cross and Death Cross detection, price-to-MA relationship scoring.

  • 03

    Macro Environment — VIX fear index, US Dollar Index (DXY), and M2 money supply trend analysis for macro tailwinds and headwinds.

  • 04

    BTC Dominance — Bitcoin's share of total crypto market cap as a signal for risk-on vs. risk-off capital rotation.

  • 05

    Network Health — Hash rate trend, mempool congestion, fee pressure, and transaction volume as on-chain demand signals.

  • 06

    Market Structure — Higher highs / lower lows pattern detection, support and resistance level proximity scoring.

  • 07

    Volume Profile — Volume relative to 20-day average, accumulation vs. distribution pattern detection.

  • 08

    Fear & Greed — Composite sentiment analysis incorporating social signals, search trends, and market momentum.

  • 09

    Price Momentum — Short-term and medium-term rate of change, comparing current momentum to historical percentiles.

  • 10

    AI Prediction — Machine learning price trend classification using historical OHLCV patterns.

  • 11

    Volatility Regime — ATR percentile analysis classifying current market volatility as low, normal, or elevated. High volatility above the 85th percentile blocks buy signals entirely.

Technology

THOR is built on Python 3.11 and Flask, running on a Raspberry Pi 5 served via Cloudflare Tunnel. The signal engine runs continuously, caching API responses to minimize external calls while keeping data fresh.

Data sources include CoinGecko for global market data and price history, Kraken and Binance for live price feeds and trading, FRED (Federal Reserve Economic Data) for macro indicators, and on-chain data for Bitcoin network metrics.

The platform is designed as a Progressive Web App (PWA) — it can be installed on any device and provides offline access to cached signal data. All trading activity goes through regulated exchanges; THOR never holds user funds.
